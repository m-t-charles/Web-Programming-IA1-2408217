Reflections on "Twelve Pillars" by Jim Rohn and Chris Widener

Twelve Pillars: Book Reflections

The following reflections are based on my reading of Chapters 4-7 of "Twelve Pillars" by Jim Rohn and Chris Widener. Each chapter summary includes key insights and practical applications to my personal and academic life.

Chapter 4: Achieve Your Goals

Chapter Summary

This chapter aptly named achieve your goals covers two friends, Micheal and Charlie, speaking causally in a diner. Charlie then gives advice to Micheal to know his goals in life as it will help him feel accomplished in life. Having his goals as motivation would help him to not feel stuck in his career. His advice comes from his own hero Mr. Davis.

Personal Application

I, like Micheal, had no written down goals just knew that I wanted to do something. The advice given made me think what i really wanted to do and starting writing short term and long term goals. Such as what I wanted to do in university and where I wanted to work after it.

Chapter 5: The Proper Use of Time

Chapter Summary

Charlie then goes on to say that time management is key. Pointing out that everyday equates to less time to soend in life so you should spend it wisely. Choose to spend your time on the things you want as well and not just the urgent things in life else you won't live life fully

Personal Application

I often spend more time on my hobbies due to spending my high-school time on the urgent; like all the sbas and CSEC requirements. Now this leaves me slightly beind in my university. So like Micheal, I have to work on a balance, to do that I've made a schedule for all my assignments.

Chapter 6: Surround Yourself With the Best People

Chapter Summary

This chapter reflects on friendships and how the people around you affect your life. Micheal's friends keep a negative attitude on most things in their life The lesson being to surrround yourself with like-minded people.

Personal Application

In this instance I've already gotten and practiced this advice. My friedns all what the same as me, to higher themselves and seeing them definitely pushes me to do the same.

Chapter 7: Be a Life-Long Leaner

Chapter Summary

This chapter speaks how one's learning is never truly done. You learn from life experiences, from those around and of course from school. This type of learning and education can only improve your life especially if your open to it all your life.

Personal Application

This is advice I've heard myself time and time again. My learnign in school isn't over and education in general never will be. Like everyoine else you will continue to experience and learn your whole life, as will I.
